# AI-Powered Competitive Intelligence & Market Research Landscape (2025–2026)

The competitive intelligence (CI) and market research software market has entered a new phase of AI-native transformation. According to the [Crayon 2025 State of CI Report (8th Edition)](https://www.crayon.co/state-of-ci), **AI usage in CI jumped 76% year-over-year**, and 56% of CI professionals now use AI in their work, with 60% using it daily. The same report notes that **55% of companies say they have more competitive deals than they did a year ago**, and sellers face competitors in nearly 7 out of every 10 deals. This pressure has driven a 22% increase in CI budgets over $25K compared to 2024, and 54% of companies now have a dedicated CI platform (up 42% from last year) — these teams report 79% higher sales effectiveness.

This report profiles the five leading AI-powered CI and market research platforms in 2025–2026, with brief honorable mentions for additional notable products.

---

## 1. Crayon

### Core Capabilities and AI Features

Crayon is a competitive intelligence platform built for B2B sales and marketing teams to monitor competitors, enable sellers, and win more deals. Its platform is organized around four pillars: **Analyze** (competitor monitoring with AI news summarization, importance scoring, automated inbox insights), **Content Creation** (battlecards, announcements, newsletters), **Sales Enablement** (intelligence delivered in Salesforce, Slack, and other tools, with team leaderboards and 1:1 coaching), and **Performance Metrics** (win/loss analysis, engagement data, influenced revenue reporting).

Crayon monitors competitor websites for changes (pricing, messaging, product pages), aggregates news and social mentions, and applies AI summarization to translate raw monitoring into actionable insights. Its standout AI feature, **"Sparks"**, has become central to how customers consume CI. In 2026, Crayon also became the first CI vendor to launch an MCP (Model Context Protocol) Server, enabling deeper integration with AI assistants.

Customer outcomes on Crayon's homepage include a **40% increase in battlecard adoption** (Alteryx), **$6 million in influenced revenue in under a year** (Cognism), a **22% increase in competitive win rate** (Salsify), and a **win rate jump from 16% to 45%** (Affinity).

### Pricing Tiers

Crayon does **not** publish list pricing; all deals are quote-based and sales-led. Per [Vendr's analysis of 92 deals](https://www.vendr.com/marketplace/crayon) (last updated February 2026):

- **Median annual contract value:** $29,500 (range $12,600–$46,500+)
- **Average savings:** 20.19%

Three tiers are commonly negotiated:

- **Essentials** — entry-level, limited competitor tracking, basic analytics (~$15,000/year for 5–10 seats, 5–10 competitors)
- **Professional** — 10–25 competitors, advanced analytics, battlecard automation, CRM integrations
- **Enterprise** — 25+ competitors, custom integrations, API access, dedicated CSM, professional services ($100,000+/year)

Hidden costs that can add 15–30% to base pricing include onboarding/professional services (5–15% of ACV), battlecard customization, API access, additional competitor profiles, and annual price escalation clauses (typically 3–7%).

### Primary Target Customers

Enterprise and mid-market B2B SaaS companies with dedicated CI/PMM teams. Notable customers include Alteryx, Cognism, Salsify, Allego, Reputation, Affinity, Archer, Bloomerang, and Oyster.

### Key Differentiators

- Acquired by **SoftwareOne for $1.4B in July 2025**, creating a combined company operating in 70 countries with ~13,000 employees
- Named a **Leader in Gartner's inaugural Magic Quadrant for Competitive and Market Intelligence (CMI) Platforms (May 2026)**
- Won 3 consecutive PMA Pulse awards (2021–2023)
- Real-time alert strength across 100+ data types
- Native integrations with Salesforce, Slack, Gong, Highspot, ChatGPT, Glean, and Microsoft Copilot

### Official Website

[https://www.crayon.co](https://www.crayon.co)

## 2. Klue

### Core Capabilities and AI Features

Klue is a competitive enablement platform that combines win-loss analysis with competitive intelligence, bringing together objective buyer feedback. The platform operates in five stages: **Collect** (track competitive landscape with unlimited competitor coverage and a browser extension), **Analyze** (triage intel and add context), **Create** (battlecards, competitor profiles, executive dashboards), **Distribute** (deliver via Slack, Teams, Salesforce, email), and **Measure** (track content adoption, revenue impact, competitive landscape changes).

The standout AI capability is **Compete Agent**, an agentic AI system launched in 2025–2026 that delivers real-time competitive deal intelligence directly to sellers during active deals. Compete Agent includes a Research Analyst (continuous intel collection and competitor profile generation), a Competitive Deal Assistant (monitors competitive activity across open opportunities), Auto Insights, "Ask Klue" (instant answers in Slack, Salesforce, and Klue), Deal Tips (deal-specific insights to inboxes), and AI-generated Competitor Profiles. Klue also has **Auto Insights** that auto-generate competitive insights from CRM data, call recordings, win-loss interviews, and trusted external sources.

Klue's acquisitions of DoubleCheck (2022, win-loss), Goldpan.ai (2025, AI-driven win-loss research), and Ignition (2026, agentic product-marketing platform) have productized win-loss in a way no other CI vendor has matched.

Customer-reported outcomes on [Klue's homepage](https://klue.com) include a **35% increase in win rate**, **55% increase in average deal size**, and **75-day reduction in average sales cycle**. Dell Technologies reported expanding competitor coverage from 12 to 54 companies with the same team.

### Pricing Tiers

Klue does **not** publish pricing — all contracts are custom, sales-led, and require a demo. No free trial is available.

Per [Vendr's analysis of 82 deals](https://www.vendr.com/marketplace/klue) (last updated February 2026):

- **Average contract value:** $30,000
- **Average savings:** 17.63%

Deployment sizing:

- **Small teams** (5–15 users) typically pay mid-five figures (Essentials)
- **Mid-sized teams** (15–50 users) pay low-to-mid six figures (Professional)
- **Large deployments** (50+ users) reach mid-to-high six figures annually (Enterprise)

Per [SelectHub](https://www.selecthub.com/p/competitive-intelligence-software/klue/), pricing starts at $16,000 annually (quote-based). Hidden costs include onboarding (low-to-mid five figures), custom integrations, premium support, data source fees, mid-contract user expansion charges, and 5–10% annual renewal escalations.

### Primary Target Customers

B2B sales-led organizations with dedicated CI, product marketing, or sales enablement teams. Customers include Cisco, 3M, Drift, SAP, Dell Technologies, Blackbaud, Gainsight, Awardco, and Relias. The platform serves 250,000+ users and is best suited for medium-to-large businesses with dedicated CI or sales enablement functions.

### Key Differentiators

- Named a **Leader in Gartner's Magic Quadrant for CMI Platforms (May 2026)**
- Highest battlecard quality rating in the category (9.5/10 on G2)
- **94% user satisfaction** rating based on 461 aggregated reviews
- Recognized as a G2 Grid Leader, Momentum Leader, Easiest To Do Business With, Best Support, and Most Likely to Recommend (Spring 2025)
- Named to Deloitte's Fast 50 and Technology Fast 500 for four consecutive years (2021–2024)
- Strong Performer in Forrester's 2024 Market and Competitive Intelligence Wave Report
- Deep productized win-loss capabilities unmatched in the category

### Official Website

[https://klue.com](https://klue.com)

## 3. AlphaSense

### Core Capabilities and AI Features

AlphaSense is an AI-driven market research and intelligence platform that indexes more than **500 million premium documents**, including company filings, expert call transcripts, broker research, and market data across 37+ languages. Its **Smart Synonyms** technology automatically expands searches to include related terms, and its sentence-level citations reduce hallucinations while enabling one-click verification.

Recent AI platform enhancements include **Generative Search** (with Deep Research mode), **Generative Grid**, **Deep Research**, **Financial Data**, and an **AI Agent Interviewer**. The platform now offers AI workflow agents that function as domain-specific analysts, with coverage of 17,000+ companies, 115+ sector comp sets, 950,000+ M&A deals, and 685,000+ funding rounds. EU data residency is available for GDPR compliance.

AlphaSense is trusted by 7,000+ enterprises worldwide, including over 85% of the S&P 100 and 70% of the top 50 hedge funds. Notable customers include Cisco, Dow, Goldman Sachs, Microsoft, Salesforce, Siemens, Pfizer, and J.P. Morgan. The company was [named to Forbes' 2025 Cloud 100 list](https://www.alpha-sense.com/blog/alpha-sense-news/inc-best-in-business-2025/) and Inc.'s 2025 Best in Business list in "Best AI Implementation" and "Best in Innovation" categories. It was also positioned highest on Ability to Execute and furthest on Completeness of Vision in Gartner's inaugural CMI Magic Quadrant (May 2026).

### Pricing Tiers

AlphaSense does **not** publish public pricing; all plans require contacting sales for a custom quote. Annual subscriptions only.

Two main plans:

- **Market Intelligence** — company documents, broker and independent research (equity research from 1,000+ sell-side and independent firms), expert transcript library, news, regulatory content, 24/7 live help, dedicated account manager support, training, SaaS hosting
- **Enterprise Intelligence** — adds internal content integration, IT support, professional services, custom training programs, and private cloud hosting options

Product add-ons include **Expert Calls** and **Canalyst financial models**.

Per [SpendHound](https://spendhound.com/vendor/alpha-sense) (last updated June 25, 2026):

- **Average SMB pricing:** $44,754/year
- **Average enterprise pricing:** $125,124/year
- SMB pricing increased 17.81% YoY; enterprise pricing increased 48.36% YoY

Per [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/alphasense/), pricing is typically $10,000–$20,000 per seat annually, with average deal sizes of $50,000–$100,000+ and enterprise deals exceeding $1M. ARR per customer grew from $28K to $66K in less than three years.

### Primary Target Customers

Financial analysts, corporate strategy teams, IR professionals, consultants, and market intelligence roles. Primary sectors include Financial Services (Investment Banking, Hedge Funds, Private Equity, Asset Management, Venture Capital), Corporations (Life Sciences & Healthcare, Tech/Media/Telecom, Energy, Industrials, Consumer Goods & Retail), and Consulting & Professional Services.

### Key Differentiators

- Recently surpassed **$500 million in ARR**, with Sacra estimating $600M ARR for March 2026 (73% YoY growth)
- **Valuation of $7.5 billion** (June 2026), up from $4 billion at Series E in June 2024
- Total funding exceeds $1.4 billion, with a $350M round led by Vitruvian Partners, Accenture Ventures, and J.P. Morgan Asset Management in June 2026
- Listed as No. 8 in the [CNBC Disruptor 50 in 2025](https://www.alpha-sense.com/blog/alpha-sense-news/cnbc-disruptor-50-2025/)
- Key acquisitions: **Tegus ($930M, July 2024)** for expanded expert transcripts, Sentieo (2022), Stream (2021), and Carousel (2025) for AI-native Excel modeling with 4,500 Canalyst models
- The only platform combining public and private financial data with expert call transcripts, broker research, and news in one workflow
- Tegus transcript library contains 100K–200,000+ transcripts covering 25,000 companies
- Enterprise Intelligence deal volume grew 185% YoY and is on track to grow 6x

## 4. Contify

### Core Capabilities and AI Features

Contify is an AI-native Market & Competitive Intelligence (M&CI) platform that delivers decision-ready insights about markets, industries, competitors, and key accounts. It provides access to over **1 million editorially curated sources**, including news, company websites, SEC filings, social media, regulatory portals, review sites, and job boards.

The platform's core AI engine is **Athena AI**, an agentic insights system with role-specific "Athena Agents" and "Ask Athena" for ad-hoc research. Contify uses a **Knowledge Graph** to extract and connect discrete data points and LLMs to derive actionable insights that address stakeholders' Key Intelligence Questions (KIQs).

Coverage spans **700,000+ companies**, **100+ industry segments**, and **200,000+ non-English sources** auto-translated from **117+ languages** — the broadest multilingual coverage in the CI category. The platform retains two years of historical data for trend analysis and applies a 7-step AI curation process to filter noise.

Five primary use cases include strategic decision-making, competitive product analysis, marketing strategy optimization, sales strategy support (battlecards, win-loss, account triggers), and role-specific insight sharing via AI. Internal source integration includes SharePoint, Slack, MS Teams, Email, Gong, and Fireflies. Native integrations include Slack, MS Teams, Salesforce, HubSpot, and Power BI.

Contify is AICPA SOC 2 certified, GDPR compliant, and supports SSO. Named a **Visionary in the 2026 Gartner Magic Quadrant for CMI Platforms**.

### Pricing Tiers

Contify does **not** publish list pricing; all deals are custom and quote-based. A **7-day free trial** is available.

Per [Improvado](https://improvado.io/blog/best-competitive-intelligence-tools), market intelligence hubs typically run $1,000–$2,500/month. Per [Debriefing](https://debriefing.io/blog/contify-pricing), mid-market pricing starts at ~$500/month, with mid-tier plans typically running $1,000–$3,000/month for teams of 5–10. Enterprise contracts typically range from $25,000–$70,000/year.

### Primary Target Customers

Mid-market companies with limited CI budgets; strategy, marketing, and business development teams; organizations needing complex intelligence routing across multiple business units; and teams prioritizing automated stakeholder briefings. Strongest sector presence in BFSI, pharma, IT, and consulting.

Notable clients include TSMC, Wipro, Merck, EY, Boston Consulting Group, Lenovo, BioCryst, Groww, Mahindra Comviva, Digital Realty, Galp, Esri, LexisNexis, and Northern Trust.

### Key Differentiators

- **Custom taxonomy system** — Contify's most distinctive feature — allowing teams to define custom tags, categories, and routing rules to direct intelligence to different stakeholders across business units
- **117+ languages** — broadest multilingual coverage in the category
- Athena AI engine launched in 2025, distinguishing it from retrofitted AI features at competitors
- 15+ years of historical data with deep archival coverage
- Managed services offering with bundled analyst hours for custom reporting
- Recognized 4 times by Gartner, plus Frost & Sullivan and Forrester
- Rated 4.5 on G2 based on 100+ reviews; G2 High Performer; SCIP member
- Typical deployment: 2 weeks setup time
- Customer outcomes: **$83M deal secured** by sales at a large IT company using Contify insights, **25 hours/week saved per analyst**, **30% reduction in manual research**, **50% improvement in competitor awareness**, **40% improvement in sales win rate**

## 5. Kompyte (by Semrush)

### Core Capabilities and AI Features

Kompyte is a competitive intelligence and sales enablement platform that automates competitor tracking across digital channels. It is primarily used by product marketing and sales enablement teams to centralize intelligence, create battlecards, and distribute them to sales.

Kompyte visits every competitor website and digital profile (social media, review sites, job postings, PR sites) once daily, using **supervised AI trained over 8 years** to classify changes and filter noise. The platform captures **500+ million data points** including competitor websites, Google News, PRNewswire, Glassdoor/Indeed/LinkedIn job postings, G2/Capterra/TrustRadius reviews, app stores, SERP/SEO monitoring, AdWords, and Crunchbase.

Features include unlimited battlecards integrated with HubSpot and Salesforce, two-way Salesforce integration for win/loss analysis, InMail win-loss interviews, and **GPT Auto Summarize** for instant takeaways. Subsequent updates added AI-powered battlecard auto-writing, IcebergIQ win/loss interview integration with GPT summaries, and support for tracking up to **200 companies** with SSO and one-click dashboards.

Kompyte was acquired by Semrush and is now positioned as the CI and sales enablement arm of Semrush.

### Pricing Tiers

Kompyte does **not** publish pricing; all contracts are custom and sales-led. Per [Autobound](https://www.autobound.ai/blog/best-competitive-intelligence-tools), most affordable pricing starts from $300/year (likely a base/limited plan for very small teams). Per [Parano.ai](https://parano.ai/competitive-intelligence-tools/), Kompyte is the cheapest of the enterprise CI suites at comparable deployment sizes, with entry deployments starting at $15,000–$20,000+/year for typical use cases. Pricing is per-competitor and per-user, which can become costly as teams scale.

### Primary Target Customers

**Lean PMM teams** (1–2 people) at mid-market companies. Kompyte is positioned as a "Best Value" pick with a price-friendly entry point, strong capture capabilities, and a 24-hour setup time. Customers tend to outgrow it as their CI programs mature.

### Key Differentiators

- Founded in 2014 in Barcelona; acquired by Semrush for ~$10M
- **Cheapest of the major enterprise CI suites** at entry tier
- Mature AI, fast setup (24 hours), and recent Semrush backing
- Customers report **30% average increase in win rates**
- Strong fit for sales-enablement-only use cases with a dedicated curator

### Official Website

[https://www.kompyte.com](https://www.kompyte.com)

## Key Market Takeaways

1. **AI adoption in CI is now mainstream.** Per Crayon's 2025 State of CI Report, 56% of CI professionals use AI in their work, with 60% using it daily — and teams deploying AI see an 82% boost in sales effectiveness.

2. **The market is consolidating around a few leaders.** Crayon (acquired by SoftwareOne for $1.4B), Klue, and AlphaSense are the three most prominent platforms, all named as Leaders in Gartner's inaugural CMI Magic Quadrant (May 2026).

3. **Budgets are rebounding.** CI budgets increased 22% YoY for budgets over $25K, with 54% of companies now operating dedicated CI platforms.

4. **Agentic AI is the next frontier.** Klue's Compete Agent, Contify's Athena AI, and AlphaSense's AI Agent Interviewer represent the shift from AI-assisted search to autonomous, workflow-embedded intelligence delivery.

5. **Most enterprise CI platforms remain quote-based.** Of the five products profiled, none publish list pricing, reflecting the customization and integration complexity typical of enterprise CI deployments.

6. **Mid-market options exist at lower price points.** Contify and Kompyte offer more accessible entry pricing for leaner teams, though feature depth and AI sophistication vary accordingly.
